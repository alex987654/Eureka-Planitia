### Notes

- Memorial station names (in italics) are the informal names given to successful US landing sites: Mutch (Viking 1), Soffen (Viking 2), Sagan (Pathfinder), Columbia (Spirit), Challenger (Opportunity), Bradbury Landing (Curiosity), Octavia E. Butler Landing (Perseverance).
- Coordinates are planetocentric latitude, +East longitude (verified against NSSDCA/NASA values for all modern landing sites).
- Soviet-era coordinates (Mars 2, 3, 6) and the Mars Polar Lander / Deep Space 2 sites are approximate and their original ocentric/ographic convention is unspecified; the positional uncertainty (tens of km) exceeds the difference between conventions.
- Ingenuity landed attached to Perseverance and is not counted as a separate landing attempt; the same applies to the Prop-M mini-rovers carried by Mars 2 and Mars 3.
- All dates are UTC, not launching-country local time. Where the local convention shifts the calendar date: Curiosity landed Aug 5 PDT (Aug 6 UTC); Spirit and Opportunity landed Jan 3 and Jan 24 PST (Jan 4 and Jan 25 UTC); Opportunity launched July 7 EDT (July 8 UTC); Tianwen-1 landed May 15 Beijing time (May 14 UTC) - Chinese announcements cite May 15.
- No landing attempts have occurred since Tianwen-1 (May 2021) as of July 2026.
